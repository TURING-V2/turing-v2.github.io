*Anger — Contempt — Disquiet — Apprehension — Seething.*

*The season I added to my life.*

You will find a rich vocabulary for every kind of feeling. None of it describes this. I had to write it myself because nothing already written would do it. It was growing into a new rhythm, everything else in my life moved to its tempo. Possibilities were endless. But I had to choose somewhere to set down, somewhere this priceless thing could take a shape I could hold. The gap between us had its own gravity. I kept returning to it the way a tongue finds a loose tooth, not for the pleasure not for the pain, but because the strangeness was irresistible. We did not fit. We went on not fitting until the not fitting began to feel like something. There is a particular pull to things you cannot explain. I could not explain her. Perhaps that was the whole problem. Perhaps that was the whole point. Somewhere between the first joke and the fourth misunderstanding, the distance became the reason to return. It did occur to me, why hold it, why try to keep it the way it is? But it was eating us from the inside. Someone had to do something, or it would become a story I'd tell later, lifelong. Somewhere inside that place we were stuck, a voice kept saying stop, deny it. It was never that different. Too weak to obey. Too persistent to ignore.

That decision, the one I made because of that voice brought me to this page.

So I write ...

I am drawing a map of someone already on someone else's map. She is in a queue I cannot enter, she is in a catalogue I was never listed in. Arranged, she calls it, with a laugh, as if the word itself is slightly embarrassing. I have been annotating the margins of a document I was not meant to read. T.S. Eliot wrote we are all born in moral stupidity, taking the world as an udder to feed our supreme selves. Maybe my supreme self has been feeding on her hesitations, mistaking them for openings. Extracting meaning they were not designed to carry.
